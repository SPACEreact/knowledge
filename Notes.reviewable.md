## SECTION 05 — Open Gaps

Open gaps are deliberate absences: you remove certainty, leave pressure, and let the audience complete meaning.

### Gap 01 — Information Gap
**Framework:** Withhold one critical fact while planting enough evidence for the audience to build a temporary truth.  
**Example:** We never see who sent the bloodstained voicemail—only waveform, timestamp, and Mira’s reaction.  
**Directing / Story Application:** Shoot partial clues (screen glow, torn note, interrupted call), then delay confirmation one scene so tension lives inside inference.

### Gap 02 — Emotional Gap
**Framework:** Show behavior before confession; let the body reveal what dialogue refuses.  
**Example:** Mira says, “I’m fine,” while aligning pencils into perfect rows and missing obvious questions from her partner.  
**Directing / Story Application:** Block contradiction inside still frames—controlled hands, unstable breath, held eye-line—to make subtext carry the beat.

### Gap 03 — Moral Gap
**Framework:** Present two defensible choices where either move violates something sacred.  
**Example:** Mira can leak caller data to save a victim now, or protect protocol and risk arriving too late.  
**Directing / Story Application:** Stage the decision in real time (no score, no cutaway comfort), then show immediate consequence in the next beat so ethics become cinematic pressure.
