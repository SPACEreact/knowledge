Editing

"What does this sequence say?"

Editing is where meaning is born. Control of time and sequence. Cinematography captures moments; editing creates meaning between them. This is not about transitions ‚Äî it's about thought architecture.

Same face + bowl of soup = hunger. Same face + dead child = grief. Same face + woman = lust. The shot doesn't contain meaning ‚Äî the juxtaposition creates it. This is the foundation of cinema.

B ¬∑ Pacing & Rhythm

When to cut is as important as where. Time is elastic.

The Blink

Walter Murch: cut on the blink. When the brain naturally blinks, it's ready for new information. Cutting at the "wrong" moment creates unease. At the "right" moment ‚Äî invisible flow.

Tension Through Rhythm

Fast cuts = anxiety, energy, chaos. Long takes = contemplation, dread, intimacy. Varying rhythm creates waves. Constant rhythm becomes noise.
