"Does this feel physically real?"

Cinematography is writing with light and space. How reality is photographed. Cinematography stops when the clip ends ‚Äî meaning is created later, in editing. This is physics, optics, and controlled chaos.

A

Light Physics

Light is the only thing the camera sees. Everything else is inference.

Three-Point Lighting

Key light creates the main illumination. Fill light softens shadows. Back light separates subject from background. This isn't a formula ‚Äî it's vocabulary. Learn it, then improvise.

Hard vs Soft Light

Hard light = small source = harsh shadows = drama. Soft light = large source = gentle shadows = beauty. The sun at noon is hard. The sun through clouds is soft. Control shadow edge, control mood.

Color Temperature

Tungsten is warm (3200K). Daylight is cool (5600K). Mixed lighting creates tension. Michael Mann uses it to separate worlds. Fincher controls it obsessively.

Motivated Lighting

Every light needs a reason. If you can't point to the source, you're lying to the audience.

Practical Lights

Lamps, candles, TVs, windows ‚Äî lights visible in frame. They justify your lighting setup. Deakins often builds entire scenes around practicals. It's believable because it's "real."

Camera Optics

Lenses don't capture reality ‚Äî they interpret it. Each focal length tells a different lie.

Focal Length Psychology

Wide lenses (24mm) exaggerate space, create intimacy or unease. Telephoto (85mm+) compresses space, isolates subjects, feels voyeuristic. 50mm is "normal" ‚Äî closest to human vision.

Depth of Field

Shallow DOF (blurry background) isolates subjects, feels cinematic. Deep DOF (everything sharp) feels documentary, democratic. Orson Welles loved deep focus. Wong Kar Wai loves shallow.

Exposure Control

The triangle of light: aperture, shutter, ISO. Master this or master nothing.

The Exposure Triangle

Aperture controls depth. Shutter controls motion blur. ISO controls sensitivity (and noise). Change one, compensate with another. It's a negotiation.

Blocking & Camera Movement

Where actors move and where camera follows. Choreography without dance.

Motivated Camera Movement

Every dolly, pan, tilt needs emotional justification. Unmotivated movement is noise. Kubrick's steadicam in The Shining is supernatural pursuit. Without reason, it's just smooth footage.
