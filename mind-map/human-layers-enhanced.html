Human Layers — Enhanced Knowledge Edition

This companion file contains the expanded material layer: deepened Enneagram notes, camera-language mappings, Podlie force mapping, and worked arc examples.

1 · Deepened Enneagram Notes
TypeCore fear / desireShadow behavior under stressArc movement to maturity
Type 9Fear of disconnection, desire for inner peace.Numbs conflict, procrastinates truth, merges with stronger personalities.Moves from passivity to presence; chooses a position and holds it through discomfort.
Type 8Fear of being controlled, desire for autonomy and strength.Over-controls people/situations, confuses tenderness with weakness.Learns protective power without domination; reveals vulnerability with discernment.
Type 7Fear of pain/deprivation, desire for freedom and stimulation.Escapes grief through novelty, fragments focus, avoids stillness.Practices depth over distraction; can stay with pain long enough for meaning.

3 · Podlie Force Mapping
ForceBody sensation firstCinematic signal setNarrative function
BhramWrongness before explanation.Near-familiar spaces, slight asymmetry, delayed sync in sound cues.Seeds doubt about reality and intention.
AnatamPull toward stillness / void.Long held shots, reduced ambient texture, narrowed tonal range.Creates introspective gravity and identity collapse risk.
XangarHeat, velocity, destabilizing surge.Aggressive cuts, directional motion conflict, high-contrast color accents.Drives rupture, confrontation, and irreversible turns.
4 · Worked Arc Examples

Arc Example A — Type 9 protagonist under Bhram pressure

Act I: avoids conflict by mirroring everyone. Act II: repeated "almost-correct" environments trigger cognitive dissonance. Act III: chooses one truth publicly, breaking the merge pattern.

Camera progression: static mids → occluded wides → centered close-up with no escape headroom.

Arc Example B — Type 8 protector under Anatam exposure

Act I: controls all variables to prevent vulnerability. Act II: isolation removes external enemies; internal wound surfaces. Act III: protects through honesty instead of force.

Camera progression: dominant low angles → balanced eye level → still, intimate close framing.

Arc Example C — Type 7 escapist under Xangar acceleration

Act I: outruns grief through motion and novelty. Act II: velocity amplifies consequences and relational damage. Act III: stays in one room, names the pain, and chooses one commitment.

Camera progression: kinetic handheld + whip pans → fragmented cutting → locked, sustained take.
